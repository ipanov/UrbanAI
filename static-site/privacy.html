Privacy Policy

Last updated: January 15, 2025

Privacy-First Approach

UrbanAI is designed with privacy at its core. We use OAuth-only authentication and store no personal identifiable information (PII). Your privacy is not just protected—it's built into the architecture of our platform.

1. Legal Basis for Processing

Under the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), we process personal data based on the following legal grounds:

  • Legitimate Interest (Article 6(1)(f)): For providing civic technology services and improving urban governance
  • Consent (Article 6(1)(a)): For optional features like location sharing and analytics
  • Performance of Contract (Article 6(1)(b)): For delivering the core reporting service
  • Public Interest (Article 6(1)(e)): For facilitating public urban issue reporting

2. Information We Collect

2.1 Identity and Authentication Data

We implement a zero-PII architecture. When you authenticate via OAuth providers:

  • OAuth Identifier: Cryptographically hashed unique identifier (not your email, name, or profile)
  • Authentication Tokens: Temporary access tokens, encrypted using AES-256, automatically expire within 24 hours
  • Session State: Minimal session data for service continuity
  • No Profile Data: We explicitly reject and do not store any personal profile information from OAuth providers

Data Minimization Principle: We collect only the absolute minimum data required for service functionality. Your email, name, and profile information are never requested, stored, or processed by our systems.

2.2 Issue Report Data

When you submit urban issue reports, we process:

  • Issue Content: Description, category, severity level (voluntarily provided)
  • Geolocation Data: Approximate location coordinates (only if explicitly consented to, with precision limited to protect privacy)
  • Media Attachments: Photos or documents you choose to upload (automatically processed to remove EXIF metadata)
  • Temporal Data: Report timestamp and processing status
  • Anonymous Linkage: Reports are linked to anonymous user sessions, not personal identities

2.3 Technical and Analytics Data

For service operation, security, and improvement:

  • Service Analytics: Aggregated usage patterns, performance metrics (anonymized and stripped of personal identifiers)
  • Security Logs: Access patterns for abuse prevention (IP addresses temporarily retained for security purposes)
  • Error Diagnostics: Technical error logs for service improvement (personal data excluded)
  • Browser Data: User agent strings and preferences for service optimization

2.4 Special Categories of Personal Data

We do not intentionally collect sensitive personal data. However, issue reports may inadvertently contain sensitive information. In such cases:

  • Processing is based on explicit consent or substantial public interest
  • Additional safeguards are implemented for data protection
  • Data subjects may request removal of sensitive content

3. How We Process Your Information

3.1 Primary Processing Purposes

  • Service Delivery: Enabling secure submission and tracking of urban issue reports
  • Municipal Integration: Anonymously forwarding reports to relevant local authorities via secure APIs
  • AI-Powered Classification: Using our multi-agent system to categorize issues and identify appropriate authorities
  • Regulatory Discovery: Matching issues with relevant municipal regulations and compliance requirements
  • Document Generation: Creating formal complaint documents when requested

3.2 Secondary Processing Activities

  • Service Enhancement: Analyzing anonymized usage patterns to improve functionality
  • Security Monitoring: Detecting and preventing platform abuse and fraudulent reports
  • Compliance Monitoring: Ensuring municipal response rates and public service accountability
  • Research and Development: Improving AI classification accuracy and system performance

3.3 Automated Decision-Making

Our AI agents perform automated processing for:

  • Issue Classification: Automatically categorizing reports (with human oversight available)
  • Authority Routing: Determining appropriate municipal departments or agencies
  • Regulatory Matching: Identifying applicable laws and regulations
  • Priority Assessment: Evaluating urgency based on public safety and environmental factors

Right to Human Review: You may request human review of any automated decision affecting your reports.

We never:

  • Sell your data to third parties
  • Use your data for advertising
  • Share personal information with municipal authorities
  • Track your personal behavior across other websites

4. Data Sharing and Disclosure

4.1 Municipal Authority Sharing

Issue reports are transmitted to relevant authorities in fully anonymized form:

  • Report Content: Issue description, category, severity assessment
  • Location Data: Approximate coordinates (precision reduced for privacy)
  • Supporting Evidence: Photos/documents with metadata stripped
  • Processing History: AI classification results and regulatory references
  • No Personal Identifiers: Absolutely no information linking reports to individuals

4.2 Third-Party Service Providers

We may share data with carefully vetted processors under strict contracts:

  • Cloud Infrastructure: Microsoft Azure (GDPR-compliant data processing agreement)
  • AI Processing: Azure OpenAI services (with data isolation and retention controls)
  • Authentication: OAuth providers (Google, Microsoft) receive only authentication requests
  • Analytics: Anonymized usage data for service improvement (no personal identifiers)

4.3 Legal Disclosure Requirements

We may disclose information when legally required, but our data minimization approach limits exposure:

  • Lawful Requests: Court orders, subpoenas, or regulatory investigations
  • Emergency Situations: Immediate threats to public safety or national security
  • Legal Assessment: Each request evaluated by legal counsel for necessity and proportionality
  • Data Protection: We challenge overly broad requests and notify users when legally permitted

4.4 Business Transfers

In the event of merger, acquisition, or asset sale:

  • Data would be transferred only with equivalent privacy protections
  • Users would be notified of any material changes to privacy practices
  • Options to delete data would be provided before transfer

5. Data Security and Protection

5.1 Technical Security Measures

We implement industry-leading security controls:

  • Encryption in Transit: TLS 1.3 for all communications with perfect forward secrecy
  • Encryption at Rest: AES-256 encryption for all stored data using Azure Key Vault
  • Database Security: Azure SQL Database with Advanced Threat Protection and transparent data encryption
  • Application Security: Regular security assessments, dependency scanning, and code reviews
  • Network Security: Azure Private Endpoints, Web Application Firewall, and DDoS protection
  • Identity Protection: Multi-factor authentication for administrative access

5.2 Organizational Security Measures

  • Access Controls: Role-based access with principle of least privilege
  • Staff Training: Regular privacy and security training for all personnel
  • Incident Response: Documented procedures for security breach detection and response
  • Audit Logging: Comprehensive logging of all data access and system changes
  • Vendor Management: Due diligence and contractual security requirements for all third parties

5.3 Data Location and Sovereignty

  • Primary Storage: Azure data centers in the European Union (Netherlands, Ireland)
  • Backup Storage: Geo-redundant backups within EU boundaries only
  • Processing Location: All AI processing occurs within EU data centers
  • No Non-EU Transfers: Data never leaves the European Economic Area without explicit consent and adequate safeguards

5.4 Data Retention and Deletion

We implement strict retention schedules aligned with legal requirements and data minimization:

  • Authentication Data: Session tokens expire within 24 hours; OAuth identifiers retained only during active use
  • Issue Reports: Retained for 7 years for municipal compliance and public record requirements
  • Technical Logs: Security logs retained for 1 year, diagnostic logs for 90 days
  • Analytics Data: Anonymized immediately upon collection, aggregated data retained for 2 years
  • Backup Data: Automated deletion following primary data retention schedule

Data Deletion Rights: You may request immediate deletion of your data at any time, subject to legitimate municipal record-keeping requirements. Environmental and public safety reports may be retained longer for regulatory compliance.

6. Your Data Subject Rights

Under GDPR, you have comprehensive rights regarding your personal data. We provide easy mechanisms to exercise these rights:

6.1 Right of Access (Article 15)

  • Data Report: Request a complete report of personal data we process about you
  • Processing Information: Details about purposes, categories, recipients, and retention periods
  • Source Information: Where we obtained your data (primarily direct from you or OAuth providers)
  • Automated Decision Details: Information about AI processing affecting your reports
  • Response Time: Within 30 days, extended to 60 days for complex requests

6.2 Right to Rectification (Article 16)

  • Data Correction: Request correction of inaccurate or incomplete personal data
  • Report Updates: Modify information in submitted issue reports (where technically feasible)
  • Verification Process: We may request evidence to verify corrections
  • Third-Party Notification: We inform municipal authorities of corrections to shared data

6.3 Right to Erasure ("Right to be Forgotten") (Article 17)

  • Complete Deletion: Request removal of all personal data from our systems
  • Grounds for Erasure: No longer necessary, consent withdrawn, unlawful processing, legal compliance
  • Municipal Records: Some issue reports may be retained for legitimate public interest or legal obligations
  • Secure Deletion: Cryptographic wiping ensuring data cannot be recovered

6.4 Right to Restrict Processing (Article 18)

  • Temporary Suspension: Limit processing while accuracy or lawfulness is verified
  • Objection Pending: Restrict processing during objection assessment
  • Limited Processing: Storage-only for legal claims or protection of rights

6.5 Right to Data Portability (Article 20)

  • Structured Export: Receive your data in machine-readable format (JSON, CSV)
  • Direct Transfer: Request direct transmission to another service provider
  • Scope: Applies to data processed by consent or contract performance
  • Report History: Complete export of your issue reports and interactions

6.6 Right to Object (Article 21)

  • Legitimate Interest Processing: Object to processing based on our legitimate interests
  • Direct Marketing: Absolute right to opt-out (though we don't conduct direct marketing)
  • Scientific Research: Object to processing for research purposes
  • Automated Decision-Making: Object to AI-only decision making affecting you

6.7 Rights Related to Automated Decision-Making (Article 22)

  • Human Review: Request human oversight of AI classification decisions
  • Challenge Decisions: Contest AI determinations about issue priority or routing
  • Explanation: Receive meaningful information about the logic involved in automated processing

6.8 Right to Lodge a Complaint

You have the right to lodge a complaint with supervisory authorities:

  • EU Residents: Your national data protection authority
  • Lead Authority: Irish Data Protection Commission (our primary establishment)
  • Local Authority: The supervisory authority in your country of residence

Exercising Your Rights

Secure Portal: privacy.urbanai.site (coming soon)

Response Time: Within 30 days (may extend to 60 days for complex requests with notification)

Identity Verification: We may request verification to protect against fraudulent requests

No Fees: Rights exercises are free unless requests are manifestly unfounded or excessive

7. Cookies and Tracking Technology

7.1 Essential Cookies (No Consent Required)

We use only technically necessary cookies for core functionality:

  • Authentication Session: Secure session management (expires when browser closes)
  • Security Tokens: CSRF protection and security validation
  • User Preferences: Language, accessibility settings, UI preferences
  • Load Balancing: Technical routing for service availability

7.2 Analytics Cookies (Consent-Based)

With your explicit consent, we may use:

  • Anonymized Usage Analytics: Aggregated service improvement data
  • Performance Monitoring: Service availability and error tracking
  • A/B Testing: Feature improvement experimentation (no personal profiling)

7.3 Explicitly Prohibited

We never use:

  • Advertising Cookies: No behavioral advertising or profiling
  • Cross-Site Tracking: No tracking across other websites
  • Social Media Pixels: No Facebook, Google, or social media tracking
  • Third-Party Analytics: No Google Analytics or similar external tracking
  • Fingerprinting: No device or browser fingerprinting

7.4 Cookie Management

  • Browser Controls: You can block or delete cookies through browser settings
  • Granular Consent: Separate opt-in for different cookie categories
  • Consent Withdrawal: Easy mechanism to withdraw consent at any time
  • Service Impact: Essential cookies required for basic functionality

9.1 Age Verification

  • Account Creation: Age confirmation required during OAuth authentication
  • No Collection: We do not knowingly collect data from children under 16
  • Immediate Deletion: Any inadvertently collected children's data is immediately deleted
  • Parental Notification: If we discover underage use, we will attempt to notify parents/guardians

9.2 Educational Use

For civic education purposes in schools:

  • Teacher Supervision: Required adult supervision for classroom demonstrations
  • No Personal Accounts: Students may not create individual accounts
  • Anonymous Examples: Educational content uses only anonymized, example data
  • School Consent: Institutional consent and parental notification required

9.3 Reporting Underage Use

If you become aware of underage use:

  • Immediate Contact: Email privacy@urbanai.site immediately
  • Account Suspension: Immediate suspension pending age verification
  • Data Deletion: Complete removal of any collected data
  • Response Time: Within 24 hours of notification

10. International Data Transfers

10.1 EU-Only Processing

Primary Commitment: All personal data processing occurs exclusively within the European Economic Area.

10.2 Transfer Safeguards (Emergency Situations Only)

In extraordinary circumstances requiring data transfer outside the EEA:

  • Adequacy Decisions: Only to countries with European Commission adequacy decisions
  • Standard Contractual Clauses: EU Commission-approved SCCs for commercial transfers
  • Binding Corporate Rules: For transfers within multinational organizations

  • Public Interest: Transfers necessary for public authority tasks

10.3 Current Transfer Status

  • No Routine Transfers: Normal operations do not involve international transfers
  • OAuth Providers: Authentication occurs within provider's EU infrastructure
  • Azure Global: Data residency locked to EU regions
  • Municipal APIs: Only connect to European municipal systems

10.4 Transfer Impact Assessment

Before any future international transfer:

  • Necessity Assessment: Evaluation of transfer necessity and alternatives
  • Risk Analysis: Privacy impact assessment for destination country
  • Safeguard Implementation: Additional technical and organizational measures
  • User Notification: Clear notice of transfer purposes and safeguards
